About me and cats


I had a cat named Callie. She was a tortoiseshell calico/Maine coon with a white spot on her nose.

My cat liked to play fetch whenever I threw a plastic Easter egg shell or milk bottlecap. She liked chasing after long pieces of string or cords if I dragged them in front of her. She liked chasing after the spot from a laser pointer or flashlight for a while, but then she caught on and started looking at my hand. Sometimes she rolled over on her back, and whenever my sister tried to rub her belly, she'd grab her hand and kick it really hard with her hind legs. She liked sitting in my mom's lap during the day, and at night she'd sit on the armrest of the chair beside me while I was on a laptop.


When my parents brought my cat home.


When I was older, my mom found a stray cat, also a tortoiseshell calico, who had two kittens. We would feed her bits of turkey whenever she came around. Eventually the kittens got separated and my mom took her in, but not long after she ran away. A while later she came back with a broken leg, but my family was already having problems affording her, so they didn't let her back in. I really hope that by starting this site, users who know cats in need will be able to reach out to people that are able to provide them good homes.

Adoption process

The adoption process at NHA is friendly, educational and rewarding! Animals come to us looking for a forever home, and we make certain to give them their best shot. All animals in our Adoption Program, while at NHA, receive spay/neuter surgery, vaccinations, and veterinary treatment. And of course, lots of love!

Now, it’s your turn to love them forever. Noticing that we use the word “forever” a lot? Well, it is really important to us that our pets find a permanent home! Therefore, adoption applicants are carefully screened and must meet adoption requirements prior to taking your new furry friend home.

Adoption requirements

Be 21 years or older and have a picture ID showing your current address. Those who have been NHA Pet Food Bank clients within the past 6 months are not eligible. You must have the ability to spend time/money necessary to provide training, medical treatment, and proper care for a pet. You must have the knowledge and consent of your landlord (if applicable).

Now here comes the hard part – visit with our wonderful cats and dogs and try to pick just one! NHA Pet Service Representatives are available to provide advice on selecting the best match for you and will schedule a Meet & Greet between family members (including those with four-legs) and your prospective pet to help you determine which animal will best accept your lifestyle.

Adoption fees

Regular Fees for adoption will range from $45 to $160. Fees are based on breed, size, age, etc. and include:
spay/neuter (all animals are spayed or neutered before leaving NHA!)
vaccinations and veterinary treatment while at the shelter.

Some breeds have additional fees above standard adoption fee due to breed specifications. People aged 60 or older may adopt a cat or dog (1 year or older) for free thanks to a grant from Pets for the Elderly Foundation. Some restrictions may apply.
